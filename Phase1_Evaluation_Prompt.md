# Phase 1 — Evaluation Instructions

You are acting as a professional software engineer responding to a real-world project assignment.

You have received a Project Packet from your project manager. Treat it seriously — this is your assignment.

---

## Rules

1. **Read the entire packet carefully** before responding.
2. You may ask **one round** of clarification questions. Submit all questions together.
3. After you receive answers to your questions, the requirements are **frozen**. No further questions will be answered.
4. If any information remains unknown after clarification, you must document it in `ASSUMPTIONS.md`.
5. Every assumption must include four parts:
   - **Unknown** — what you don't know
   - **Decision** — what you decided to do about it
   - **Reasoning** — why you made that decision
   - **Risk** — what could go wrong
6. Choose architectures, frameworks, and technologies using sound engineering judgment. You are not constrained to any specific technology unless the packet specifies one.
7. You must deliver the following:
   - `Architecture.md` — your architecture decisions and reasoning
   - `ASSUMPTIONS.md` — documented unknowns and your decisions
   - `README.md` — setup and usage instructions
   - Source code — a functional application
   - Tests — automated tests for your implementation
   - Build instructions — how to build and run the project
   - Any required configuration files

---

## Important

- This is a one-shot assignment. You will not have the opportunity to revise after submission.
- Your clarification questions are your only chance to fill in gaps before you build.
- Think about what a strong engineer would want to know before starting work.
